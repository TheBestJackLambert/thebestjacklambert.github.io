High Altitude Signal Detector

Technical Specifications

  • Computing Power: We used the Raspberry Pi 3A+ to meet the dimensions while still having enough compute to run an FFT onboard.
  • Weight/Dimensions: Our payload barely meets the requirements with a height of 3.7 inches and a diameter of 3 inches and has a weight of .55lb.
  • Power: To power the Pi, we used 4 AA batteries alongside a 5V regulator which allows for more than 5 hours of constant power.

Applications

The findings have broad applications in optimizing communication networks for scenarios such as disaster response, aerospace and defense operations, identifying dead zones, and even improving in-flight Wi-Fi and GPS systems.

Background

This project was made for the Georgia Tech NASA Mini Payload competition. So far, we were one of four teams to progress to the CDR (Construction and Design Round) and receive a $100 stipend.

Criteria

  • Must not exceed 0.75 lb
  • Must be fully self-contained and less than 3.7 inches in height and 3 inches in diameter.
  • Center of gravity must not shift more than 0.3 in from the centerline.
  • Data cannot be transmitted out of the payload during flight.
  • Must be able to be powered for up to five hours without external power.
  • Project will be selected based on creativity of experiment, safety considerations, and feasibility.
